Workflow

When creating a webpage, it is super important that you know the purpose of your webpage. What are you trying to accomplish? What do you want the user to do or see? Take a look at Amazon's webpage. Their main goal is to sell products. So, on their homepage, they fill it with products relevant to you and other promotions.

Now look at the google homepage. What does google want you to do? They want you to search of course. So, all their homepage has is a search bar with other small links.

It is a really good idea to design your page before putting it into code. This will facilitate and speed up your workflow exponentially. You can do this by drawing out your page on paper or on a device and plan what you will have where.

"Design your page before putting it into code"

Flexbox and Centering

In CSS, centering is a headache! But luckily, there's some small tricks such as flexbox that can help us. Flexbox is super handy for having a flex-ible webpage. It is also useful for laying out elements horizontally or vertically using the flex-direction property. The graph and paragraph next to each other below are inside a <div> box and I said "flex-direction: row;" to position them next to each other inside the box.
